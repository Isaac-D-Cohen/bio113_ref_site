What is this?

In BIO 113 at our school, the discussion section requires submission of various written assignments over the course of the semester. Among other things, these assignments require students to format citations using a very specific referencing system made up exclusively for the BIO 113 course. The reason for this is obvious. If their goal were simply to teach material it would suffice to use APA, MLA, Chicago, or any of the other myriad, arbitrary forms of torture invented to drive students nuts over the years. But like all things in college, the purpose here is precisely to make things difficult so that students who rise to the challenge can signal their capabilities and leave their friends in the dust. I have written about this at length elsewhere and won't belabor the point here. Suffice it to say, the problem from their perspective, with these other referencing systems is that software is widely available to automate them. Thus, they make up their own referencing system in the hopes of forcing students to go through the painful work of following it by hand. Because I despise these people and their rotten goal, and in the hopes of assisting the public in defending themselves against it, I have written this little website to at least partially automate the task of formatting according to their newly made-up referencing system. I hope you enjoy using it.

--Isaac Cohen
